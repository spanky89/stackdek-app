### Don't Build:
❌ **Chart of Accounts** - Too complex for contractors

❌ **General ledger** - Overkill  
❌ **Reconciliation** - Let their accountant do it

❌ **Tax prep** - Let CPA handle it  

### Keep it Simple:
✅ **Job-level profit** - That's all they need  
✅ **Expense tracking** - Per job  
✅ **Invoice → Payment** - Track cash  
✅ **Export to CSV** - For their accountant  
✅ **Year-end P&L** - Simple report  

**Rule:** If it requires accounting knowledge, DON'T build it.

## 📱 The Contractor's Dream Workflow

### Current Reality (QuickBooks + CRM):

1. **Lead comes in** → Enter in CRM
2. **Create quote** → Manually enter in CRM
3. **Win job** → Manually create in QB
4. **Track expenses** → Paper receipts → Enter in QB later
5. **Track time** → Separate time app → Import to QB
6. **Create invoice** → Manually in QB (re-enter line items)
7. **Get paid** → Mark paid in QB AND CRM
8. **Check profit** → Run QB report (confusing)

**8 steps, 3 different apps, tons of manual entry.**

---

### StackDek Workflow:

1. **Lead comes in** → Enter client
2. **Create quote** → Send to client
3. **Win job** → Quote auto-converts to job
4. **Track expenses** → Employees snap receipts (1-tap)
5. **Track time** → Clock in/out (auto-logs to job)
6. **Create invoice** → Job auto-creates invoice (line items flow through)
7. **Get paid** → Mark paid (updates everywhere)
8. **Check profit** → See real-time on JobDetail page

**Same 8 steps, ONE app, 90% automated.**
